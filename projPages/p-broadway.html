Working At Broadway Motor and Muffler

June 2017 - August 2017 | Work Experience
During the summer following my sophomore year in highschool I worked at a local automotive repair shop. I helped out with whatever I could and was often left on my own for tasks such as changing oil and dismantling / re-assembling car components.

While working for Dave, my boss at Broadway Motor and Muffler, I learned a fair amount about what goes into making cars tick. I learned about differentials, transmissions, catalytic converters, different types of engines, measurements for bolts and more. However not all of what I learned is only applicable to cars, I also developed a superior work ethic while working for Dave. I remember coming to him and asking for help and him responding:

"I don't pay you to bring me problems, I pay you for solutions!"

Though his tone was lighthearted, this really spoke to me. His attitude during my time working there was one that promoted my ability to solve problems independently, and as a lead builder in my robotics club, I can relate with Dave. I don't have time for people to look for the easy solution by asking questions that they could probably figure out the answer to if they tried.

Sometimes while working in the garage I could relate to those who have repetitive factory jobs. There were days where all I would do is clean, sort bolts, and crush oil filters. I can't imagine staying sane doing the same task over and over day after day. One day I spent a good eight hours crushing oil filters. Every day I would come home with black hands and a face smeared with oil and god knows what else. I didn't mind, I thought it was pretty fun. I had less free time over that summer than over my sophomore year.
